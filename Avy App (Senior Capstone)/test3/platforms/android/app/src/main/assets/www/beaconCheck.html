Beacon check

  • Turn on your beacon and check the battery and lights/arrows. The battery should be at least 60% (with lithium batteries)

    • Before you leave the trailhead:


  • Make sure everyone has their beacons turned on and they have checked their batteries

  • Keep one beacon on 'send', and have everyone else turn their's to 'search'

  • With everyone at least 3 meters apart, have the person with the 'send' beacon move around to each person, ensuring that each beacon is accurately tracking direction and distance in regard to the beacon that is 'sending'

  • Switch all of the beacons. Now one is searching and the rest are sending. Have the person with the 'search' beacon start down the trail and stop at least 30 meters away

  • One at a time, have each person walk by the 'searcher'. The 'searcher' makes sure their beacon is getting an accurate signal from each passing person

  • If everyone has a properly working beacon, double check that everyone in the group is 'sending' and their beacons are secured to their bodies. Have a great adventure!

    • **there are many ways to do a beacon check, this is just one that works well with groups.
